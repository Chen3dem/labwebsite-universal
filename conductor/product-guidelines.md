# Product Guidelines: The CUI Lab Website\n\n## 1. Visual Aesthetic: Professional & Traditional\n\nThe website will adhere to a **Professional & Traditional** aesthetic, reflecting the lab's position within an established research institution (CU Anschutz).\n\n-   **Design Principles:**\n    -   **Structured Layout:** Clear, organized sections with a predictable flow.\n    -   **Balanced Color Palette:** Use of official university colors to establish authority and trust.\n    -   **Refined Typography:** Professional serif or sans-serif fonts for high readability and a serious tone.\n    -   **High-Quality Imagery:** Prioritization of authentic, high-resolution scientific and lab photos.\n\n## 2. Tone of Voice: Multi-Faceted & Professional\n\nThe content should strike a balance between several key tones to effectively reach different audiences:\n\n-   **Academic & Rigorous:** For peers and grant agencies, maintaining technical accuracy and formal language in project descriptions and publications.\n-   **Accessible & Inviting:** For prospective students and the general public, using clear and welcoming language in "About" and "Join Us" sections.\n-   **Visionary & Inspiring:** To highlight the broader impact of the lab's work, emphasizing its role in the future of science and health.\n\n## 3. Data & Information Presentation: Simple & Clear\n\nComplex information will be presented with a focus on clarity and ease of access:\n\n-   **Text-First Summaries:** Prioritize clean, easy-to-read summaries of research findings and projects.\n-   **Direct Links:** Provide prominent links to full publications, datasets, or external resources.\n-   **Structured Lists:** Use clean, filterable tables or lists for publications and team members to manage information density effectively.\n\n## 4. Imagery & Media\n\nThe website will feature a mix of authentic and representative imagery:\n\n-   **Lab Life:** Authentic, high-resolution photos of team members at work, experiments in progress, and the lab environment.\n-   **Scientific Visualizations:** High-quality microscopy, data plots, and 3D models directly related to the lab's research area.\n-   **Conceptual Graphics:** Modern illustrations or professional stock imagery that conceptually aligns with the lab's scientific focus.\n\n## 5. Branding & Identity (CU Anschutz Standards)\n\nThe website MUST strictly adhere to the **CU Anschutz Brand Identity Standards** (as of 2025):\n\n-   **Logos:**\n    -   **Primary Campus Logo:** The official CU Anschutz full-color campus logo must be the primary visual identifier and must not be altered.\n    -   **Unified Lockups:** Only approved school lockups or the campus logo are permitted. Custom-designed lab or department logos are strictly prohibited.\n    -   **Logo Usage:** Ensure proper clear space (height of the 'U') and minimum sizing (40px digital) are maintained.\n-   **Color Palette:**\n    -   **Primary:** CU Gold (approx. #CFB87C) and Black (#000000).\n    -   **Secondary/Accent:** To be selected from the approved university secondary palette (e.g., shades of gray, dark blue) once finalized.\n-   **Compliance:** Do not create custom graphic symbols for the lab. Identify the "Nassour Lab" in text alongside the official university branding.
